W: Hello, students. As you know, animals build many different structures in the natural world. Today, we'll talk about the different functions that these structures have. First, an important function is to provide protection from predators. For example, beavers build dams. Next, animal structures can play a role in transportation. Some species of ants use their own bodies to create bridges. In addition, animal structures can serve as traps. This can be observed in web-building spiders. Finally, animal structures can serve as a means of attracting mates. Some species of birds create structures that attract the attention of females.
