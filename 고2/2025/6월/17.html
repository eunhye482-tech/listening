W: Hello, students! In our last lesson, we learned how animals use their senses to find food. Today, we'll talk about ways some animals defend themselves from enemies. First, one of the most well-known of these animals is the skunk. Skunks spray a bad-smelling liquid to keep enemies away. Skunk spray does not cause any lasting damage, though its smell is hard to remove. Second, chameleons change the color of their skin to match their surroundings, making them hard to spot. That's one clever way to hide from their enemies! Next, we have frogs. By making themselves look bigger, they appear more threatening. Another animal that uses its body for survival is the lizard. Interestingly, lizards can cut off their own tails to escape. But don't worry the tail eventually grows back!
